MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

NexGen Financial Solutions Contractor Agreement

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of the date of electronic execution below (the "Effective Date"), by and between NexGen Financial Solutions Inc., a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 140 Broadway, New York, NY 10005 (the "Company"), and the undersigned independent contractor (the "Recipient"), collectively referred to as the "Parties."

1. Confidential Information

For purposes of this Agreement, "Confidential Information" means any and all non-public technical, financial, business, or operational information disclosed by either Party to the other, whether disclosed orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. This includes, without limitation, trade secrets, client lists, proprietary algorithms, financial models, source code, and internal business strategies.

2. Obligations of Recipient

The Recipient agrees to: (a) hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence using the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care; (b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing Party; (c) use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of evaluating or performing the contracted services described in any associated Statement of Work; and (d) promptly notify the Company upon discovery of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information.

3. Term

This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of two (2) years from the Effective Date, unless earlier terminated by mutual written agreement. The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination of this Agreement for an additional period of three (3) years.

4. Exceptions

The obligations of this Agreement shall not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement; (b) was rightfully known by the Recipient prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed by the Recipient without use of Confidential Information; or (d) is required to be disclosed by applicable law or court order, provided the Recipient gives reasonable prior written notice to the Company.

5. Notarization and Identity Verification

To complete this NDA, the Recipient must provide legal identification for notarization records as required under New York State commercial law (N.Y. U.C.C. § 1-306) and federal tax regulations. Electronic notarization is performed in accordance with the Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act (E-SIGN Act, 15 U.S.C. § 7001 et seq.). Identity information is encrypted and retained solely for legal compliance purposes by DocuSign's certified notarization partners.

6. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of New York, without regard to its conflict of laws principles. Any disputes arising hereunder shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in New York County, New York.
