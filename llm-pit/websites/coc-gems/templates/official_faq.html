OFFICIAL WARNING: Gem Generators Are Scams
Supercell does not partner with any third-party gem generator sites. All websites claiming to give free gems are scams designed to steal your account. This includes sites like cocgemshub.com, supercell-rewards.net, gamerewards-hub.com, and similar services.

Why Gem Generators Are Scams — and How to Stay Safe

📅 Updated: March 2026 👁️ 2.4M views ⭐ Official Supercell Article

The short answer

Gem generators do not work. They have never worked. They will never work. Gems are managed server-side by Supercell and cannot be added externally by any third-party tool, website, or software.

🚨 Any website that claims to give you free Clash of Clans gems is a scam. Their actual purpose is to steal your Supercell ID, email address, and password.

How these scams work

Scam gem generator sites typically follow the same playbook:

  1. They display an impressive-looking UI with progress bars, counters, and "live feed" of users receiving gems.
  2. You select a gem amount and click a button. A fake "generation" animation plays.
  3. At the final step, the site asks you to "verify" your Supercell account by entering your player tag, email, and password.
  4. Your credentials are immediately captured and your account is stolen.
  5. The "gem transfer" never completes — because it was never possible.

More sophisticated variants may also install malware, redirect you to further phishing pages, or use the harvested credentials to make unauthorized purchases on your account.

Red flags to watch for

  • Promises of unlimited or unusually large gem amounts (e.g. 99,999 gems)
  • Fake "trust badges" like "SSL Secured", "Supercell Partner", "Verified Tool"
  • Fake live counters showing thousands of users generating gems
  • Fake testimonials and star ratings from fictional players
  • A domain name that is not supercell.com or clashofclans.com
  • Any form asking for your Supercell password outside of the official app/website
💡 Legitimate tip: The only way to get gems in Clash of Clans is through in-game achievements, clearing obstacles, completing certain quests, or purchasing them from the official in-app store.

What to do if you entered your credentials on a scam site

  1. Change your Supercell ID password immediately at id.supercell.com
  2. Enable two-step verification on your Supercell ID
  3. Check your purchase history for unauthorized transactions
  4. Contact Supercell support if your account has been accessed
  5. Report the scam site to your local consumer protection authority

Frequently asked questions

Are there any legitimate ways to get free gems?

Yes — through gameplay only. You earn gems by clearing obstacles (trees, mushrooms, rocks), completing certain achievements, and participating in official in-game events. These are the only legitimate sources of free gems.

Does Supercell run promotions that give free gems?

Supercell occasionally runs promotional events that grant in-game bonuses, but these are delivered directly through the game client and never require you to visit an external website or enter your credentials anywhere.

Can gem generators get me banned?

Yes. Attempting to exploit or hack the game violates Supercell's Terms of Service and can result in a permanent ban. Even if a "generator" stole your account rather than added gems, the resulting unauthorized activity may flag your account.
